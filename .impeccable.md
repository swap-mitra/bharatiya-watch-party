## Design Context

### Users
Desktop watch-party hosts and viewers on macOS and Windows. The host wants to create a room, paste a direct stream URL, control playback, and keep the group in sync. Viewers want to join quickly, watch without friction, and use lightweight chat while the video remains the clear focus.

### Brand Personality
Raw, focused, restrained. The product should feel like a purposeful screening room control surface rather than a social app dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction
Neo-brutalist minimalism with a muted dark palette, hard borders, square geometry, strong type, low ornament, and no glossy gradients or vibrant color wash. Player and chat are the visual priority; secondary diagnostics and status detail should stay quiet or be removed from the main path.

### Design Principles

- Put the player first, chat second, everything else after.
- Use blunt geometry, strong borders, and compact information density instead of decorative cards.
- Keep color rare and functional; prefer ink, bone, concrete, and one muted accent.
- Keep theater mode directly attached to the player controls.
- Remove repeated status copy and nonessential panels unless they help a user act.
